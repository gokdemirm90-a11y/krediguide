# How to Compare Mortgage Lenders

Getting a mortgage is likely the biggest financial decision you'll make. Choosing the right lender can save you thousands of dollars over the life of your loan. The good news? Comparing lenders isn't as hard as it sounds.

Get Multiple Quotes

Start by gathering quotes from at least three different lenders. This includes banks, credit unions, and online lenders. When you request a quote, you'll provide basic information about your home purchase and finances. Ask each lender for a Loan Estimate form—this is a standardized document that shows all the costs upfront, making comparison easier.

Practical tip: Get all your quotes within a two-week window. Shopping around during a short timeframe won't hurt your credit score as much as spread-out applications.

Compare the Real Costs

Don't just look at interest rates. The lowest rate doesn't always mean the best deal. Compare the total cost by looking at:

- Interest rate and whether it's fixed or adjustable - Origination fees (charges for processing your loan) - Closing costs (title insurance, appraisals, underwriting fees) - Monthly payment amounts - Total interest you'll pay over the full loan term

Most Loan Estimates include all these numbers, so you can compare apples to apples.

Practical tip: Use a mortgage calculator online to see how different rates and fees affect your monthly payment. Even small differences add up over 30 years.

Check Customer Service and Reputation

The cheapest lender isn't worth it if their service is terrible. Read reviews on Google, the Better Business Bureau, and Zillow. Pay attention to comments about responsiveness, clarity, and whether the lender delivered what they promised.

Call each lender's customer service line with questions. See how quickly they respond and whether they explain things clearly. You'll work closely with your lender for several months, so choose someone you trust.

Practical tip: Ask each lender how long the approval process typically takes. This matters if you're on a timeline.

The Bottom Line

Taking time to compare lenders upfront pays off. You might save hundreds or even thousands in fees and interest. Don't rush the process—this decision affects your finances for decades. Gather quotes, compare numbers carefully, and choose a lender you feel confident working with.
