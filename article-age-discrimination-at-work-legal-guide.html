30-Year vs 15-Year Mortgage: Which Is Better?

📊 Mortgage · June 1, 2026 · 5 min read

# Choosing Your Mortgage Term: A Simple Guide

When you're ready to buy a home, one of the biggest decisions is picking between a 30-year and 15-year mortgage. Both have real advantages and real drawbacks. The right choice depends entirely on your financial situation and goals.

Monthly Payments and Your Budget

The most obvious difference is how much you pay each month. With a 30-year mortgage, your monthly payment is lower because you're spreading the debt over twice as long. A 15-year mortgage means higher monthly payments, sometimes 50% more. Before choosing, honestly evaluate what fits your monthly budget. Can you comfortably afford the higher payment on a 15-year loan? If missing even one payment would stress you out, the 30-year option might be safer.

Total Cost and Interest

Here's the catch: lower monthly payments don't mean you're paying less overall. With a 30-year mortgage, you'll pay significantly more interest over the life of the loan. On a $300,000 home at 7% interest, you could pay nearly $250,000 in interest with a 30-year loan versus $160,000 with a 15-year loan. That's a $90,000 difference. If you can afford the higher payment, the 15-year option saves serious money.

Building Equity and Peace of Mind

A 15-year mortgage builds equity in your home much faster. After just 15 years, you own your home outright. Many people find this incredibly satisfying—imagine having no mortgage payment at all in your 50s. However, the 30-year option gives you flexibility. You could make higher payments when times are good and stick to your regular payment during tough months.

Practical Tips

Start by getting pre-approved for both options. See what the actual monthly payments would be for your situation. Next, look at your other financial goals. Do you have an emergency fund? Are you saving for retirement? If you're struggling with these basics, the 30-year mortgage gives you breathing room. Also consider your job stability. Stable income? The 15-year mortgage is more comfortable.

The Bottom Line

Neither choice is universally "better." A 15-year mortgage makes sense if you have stable income, a good budget cushion, and want to save on interest. A 30-year mortgage works better if you value monthly flexibility or want to invest extra money elsewhere. Choose based on your real financial picture, not what sounds impressive.
