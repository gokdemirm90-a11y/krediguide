# How to Dispute a Medical Bill

Medical bills can be confusing and sometimes wrong. Studies show that many hospital bills contain errors. The good news? You have the right to challenge charges you believe are incorrect. Here's how to do it.

Review Your Bill Carefully

Start by understanding what you're being charged for. Request an itemized bill from your healthcare provider—this breaks down every service and supply. Compare it to your records. Look for duplicate charges, services you didn't receive, or prices that seem unreasonably high. Don't assume the bill is correct just because it came from a hospital or doctor's office. Take notes on anything that looks wrong. This step takes time but catches most errors.

Gather Your Documents

Collect everything related to your care. Keep copies of appointment confirmations, insurance paperwork, and previous medical bills. Check your insurance explanation of benefits (EOB) to see what your insurance should have paid. Call your insurance company if something doesn't match. Get the names and titles of anyone you speak with. Document all conversations with dates and times. This paper trail helps prove your case if you need to escalate your dispute.

Contact the Provider and File Your Dispute

Call your healthcare provider's billing department first. Explain which charges you're disputing and why. Most providers will investigate without needing a formal written request, but sending a letter helps create a record. Keep your letter simple: list the disputed charges, explain why they're wrong, and request a correction. Send it certified mail so you have proof of delivery.

If the provider doesn't resolve it, file a complaint with your state's health department or attorney general's office. You can also contact your insurance company to ask them to investigate. They have staff dedicated to checking medical bills.

Conclusion

Disputing a medical bill takes effort, but it's worth it. You earned the right to accurate billing. Don't ignore bills that seem wrong—errors won't disappear on their own. Start with a careful review, stay organized, and contact your provider directly. Most disagreements get resolved quickly when you ask politely but firmly. Taking action protects your wallet and helps ensure the healthcare system works fairly for everyone.
