# How to Report Workplace Harassment

Workplace harassment affects millions of Americans every year. If you're being bullied, threatened, or treated unfairly because of your race, gender, age, or other protected characteristics, you have the right to report it. Taking action can feel scary, but there are clear steps you can follow.

Document Everything

Start by keeping a detailed record of what's happening. Write down the date, time, and what was said or done. Include the names of any witnesses. Save emails, texts, or messages related to the harassment. Take screenshots if needed. This documentation becomes crucial evidence if you need to file a formal complaint.

Don't rely on memory alone. The more specific details you have, the stronger your case becomes.

Use Your Company's Reporting System

Most companies have a formal process for reporting harassment. Check your employee handbook or ask Human Resources for the complaint procedure. This usually means:

- Speaking with your HR department in person or by email - Filing a written complaint - Speaking with your direct supervisor (if they're not the harasser)

Send your complaint in writing when possible, and keep a copy for yourself. This creates an official record that your company must address.

Report to Outside Agencies If Needed

If your company doesn't help or the harassment continues, you can file a complaint with the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC). You have 180-300 days to file, depending on your state. Visit eeoc.gov or call 1-800-669-4000. Filing is free.

You can also contact your state's labor department or civil rights agency. Some situations may involve local police, especially if the harassment includes threats or physical contact.

Know Your Protections

Federal law protects you from retaliation. Your employer cannot fire you, demote you, cut your pay, or punish you for reporting harassment. If retaliation happens, that's illegal.

Moving Forward

Reporting workplace harassment takes courage, but you deserve a safe work environment. Document your experience, use your company's process first, and don't hesitate to contact outside agencies if needed. You're not alone, and help is available.
