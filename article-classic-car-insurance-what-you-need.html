# Car Insurance After a DUI

Getting arrested for driving under the influence is stressful enough. Then comes another worry: what happens to your car insurance? The good news is that you can still get coverage. The challenging news is that it will cost more and take some effort to find. Here's what you need to know.

Your Insurance Will Likely Go Up Significantly

After a DUI conviction, expect your rates to increase dramatically. Most insurers will raise your premiums by 50% to 100% or more. Some companies may refuse to cover you at all. This hit to your wallet can last for years—usually three to five years, depending on your state and insurance company.

Shop around before assuming your current insurer is your only option. Different companies view DUI convictions differently. Some specialize in high-risk drivers and may offer better rates than the company that dropped you.

Find an SR-22 or FR-44 Form

Many states require drivers with DUI convictions to file an SR-22 form (or FR-44 in Florida and Virginia). This document proves you have minimum liability insurance. You'll need to maintain it for several years—typically three to five. Your insurance company files this form for you, but you must keep paying on time. Missing a payment can have serious legal consequences.

Take Steps to Lower Your Costs

You can't undo a DUI, but you can reduce what you'll pay going forward. Consider these practical moves:

- Take a defensive driving course. Many insurers offer discounts of 5% to 10% if you complete an approved course. - Bundle policies. Combining auto, home, or renters insurance often lowers overall costs. - Increase your deductible. Choosing a higher deductible (like $1,000 instead of $500) means lower monthly premiums. - Maintain a clean record. Each year without another violation helps rebuild your driving history. - Ask about usage-based programs. Some insurers offer apps that track safe driving habits and reward good behavior with discounts.

Moving Forward

A DUI doesn't have to mean financial ruin, even though it's expensive and frustrating. Be honest with insurance companies, shop around, and focus on being a safer driver going forward. Over time, the impact on your rates will fade, and your wallet will recover.
