# Drunk Driving Accident: Your Legal Options

If you've been hit by a drunk driver, you're facing medical bills, car repairs, and serious stress. The good news is that the law gives you several ways to get compensated. Here's what you need to know.

File an Insurance Claim

Start by reporting the accident to your insurance company right away. Your own policy often covers you first, regardless of who caused the crash. The other driver's insurance company should also pay if their policyholder was at fault. Document everything: take photos of vehicle damage, get the other driver's information, and request a copy of the police report. Keep all receipts for medical care and repairs.

Pursue a Personal Injury Lawsuit

If the drunk driver's insurance won't pay enough to cover your damages, you can sue in civil court. You'll need to prove the driver was negligent (careless) and caused your injuries. A drunk driving conviction helps your case significantly, but you can still win even without one. You can seek money for medical expenses, lost wages, pain and suffering, and vehicle damage. Most personal injury lawyers work on a contingency basis, meaning they only get paid if you win.

The Dram Shop Law Option

Many states have "dram shop" laws that let you sue the bar or restaurant that served the drunk driver. If they over-served alcohol or served someone they knew was intoxicated, they may be liable. This is helpful when the driver doesn't have much insurance coverage. However, these laws vary by state, so ask your lawyer if this applies where you live.

Practical Tips

Keep detailed records of all accident-related expenses. Write down your injuries and how they've affected your daily life. Get statements from witnesses. Don't accept a settlement offer too quickly—you might not know the full extent of your injuries for weeks. Consider consulting with a personal injury attorney before talking to insurance adjusters; many offer free consultations.

Conclusion

Being hit by a drunk driver isn't your fault, and you have legal rights to compensation. Act quickly, document everything, and don't hesitate to seek legal advice. Most accidents settle without going to trial, so you could see results within months.
