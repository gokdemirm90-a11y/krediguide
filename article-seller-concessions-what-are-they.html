🤝 Mortgage · June 1, 2026 · 5 min read

# What Are Seller Concessions?

When you're buying a home, the seller might offer to pay for certain costs on your behalf. These payments are called seller concessions, and they can save you thousands of dollars at closing.

Understanding the Basics

Seller concessions are credits the seller gives to the buyer to cover closing costs or repairs. Instead of you paying these expenses out of pocket, the seller agrees to pay them as part of the purchase agreement. Common examples include paying your property inspection fees, appraisal costs, title insurance, or loan origination fees.

Think of it as a negotiation tool. You offer a higher purchase price for the home, and in return, the seller covers some of your costs. This arrangement helps both parties—you reduce your upfront expenses, and the seller gets a slightly higher sales price.

How They Work in Practice

Seller concessions typically appear in your purchase agreement as a dollar amount or percentage. For example, you might negotiate a 2-3% concession on a $300,000 home, which equals $6,000 to $9,000 in covered costs.

Lenders do have limits. Most conventional loans allow up to 3% in seller concessions, while FHA loans permit up to 6%. If you go over these limits, lenders may require the purchase price to be adjusted downward.

It's important to understand that concessions don't lower your actual purchase price. They just shift who pays certain fees—the seller instead of you.

When to Ask for Concessions

Concessions make the most sense in a buyer's market when there's less competition for homes. If you're buying during a hot seller's market, sellers have little reason to offer them.

They're also helpful if you're short on cash for closing costs or if inspections reveal needed repairs. Rather than renegotiating the price, you can ask the seller to cover repair costs through concessions.

Practical Tips

Get pre-approved for your mortgage before making an offer. Your lender can tell you exactly what closing costs you'll owe and help you calculate realistic concession requests.

Always work with your real estate agent to research similar homes in your area. This shows you what concessions are reasonable for your market.

Have your inspector and appraiser complete their work early. You'll know exact repair costs before finalizing your offer.

Moving Forward

Seller concessions are a legitimate way to reduce your out-of-pocket expenses when buying a home. They work best when you understand your lender's rules and ask for reasonable amounts. With proper planning, they can make homeownership more affordable from day one.
