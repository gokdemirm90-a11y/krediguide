# What to Do After a Car Accident: Step by Step

Car accidents are stressful, but knowing what to do immediately afterward can protect your safety and your wallet. Follow these steps to handle the situation properly.

Step 1: Make Sure Everyone Is Safe

First, check yourself and your passengers for injuries. If anyone is hurt, call 911 right away. Turn on your hazard lights and move vehicles out of traffic if possible—but only if it's safe to do so. If you can't move your car, stay inside with your seatbelt on and wait for help. Never stand in the roadway.

Step 2: Document Everything at the Scene

Once it's safe, gather information. Exchange names, phone numbers, addresses, and insurance details with the other driver. Get their license plate number and vehicle details. Take photos of all vehicle damage from multiple angles. Photograph the accident scene, road conditions, and traffic signs. If there are witnesses, get their contact information. Write down the date, time, location, and how the accident happened while it's fresh in your mind.

Step 3: Report and Follow Up

Call your insurance company within a few days and report the accident. Provide them with all the information you collected. Don't admit fault or apologize to the other driver, even if you think the accident was your fault—let insurance determine that. Keep copies of all documents related to the accident, including photos, police reports, and repair estimates.

If you need medical care, get it checked out even if you feel fine. Some injuries appear hours or days later. Keep records of all medical visits and expenses.

Key Tips to Remember

- Stay calm and polite at the scene - Never leave without exchanging information - Don't sign anything except police reports or insurance documents - Keep your insurance information in your car - Consider getting a dash cam to protect yourself in the future

Taking these steps immediately after an accident protects you legally and financially. You'll have the documentation needed for insurance claims and any potential legal issues. Stay safe, stay organized, and don't hesitate to seek professional help if needed.
