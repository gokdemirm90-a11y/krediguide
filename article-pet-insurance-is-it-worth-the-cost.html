# Pedestrian Accident: Your Legal Rights

Getting hit by a car while walking is scary and confusing. You might face medical bills, lost wages, and pain that lasts months. The good news is that the law protects you. Understanding your rights helps you recover fairly.

What You Need to Do Right After an Accident

First, focus on your safety and health. Move to a safe location if possible, then call 911 if anyone is injured. Take photos of the accident scene, the vehicle, and your injuries. Get the driver's name, phone number, address, and insurance information. Write down the license plate number. Ask nearby people for their contact information—witnesses are crucial.

File a police report, even for minor accidents. Get the report number. Report the accident to your own car insurance company (if you were a passenger) or the driver's insurance. Keep all medical records, receipts, and documentation of your injuries.

Understanding Your Legal Rights

As a pedestrian, you have the right to compensation for your injuries if the driver was careless or broke traffic laws. This includes medical expenses, lost income while recovering, and pain and suffering. You can recover money even if you were partially at fault, depending on your state's laws.

Most states follow "comparative negligence" rules. This means if you were 20% responsible and the driver was 80% responsible, you can still recover 80% of your damages. A few states are stricter and don't allow any recovery if you share any blame.

Getting Help and Moving Forward

Contact a personal injury lawyer. Many work on contingency—they only get paid if you win your case. Initial consultations are often free. A lawyer handles negotiations with insurance companies and protects your interests.

Don't accept the first settlement offer without legal advice. Insurance companies often offer less than you deserve. Document everything: medical bills, therapy costs, time missed from work, and how your injury affects daily life.

Conclusion

Pedestrian accidents are serious, but you're not alone. Your rights are protected by law. Gather evidence, report the accident, seek medical care, and consult a lawyer. Taking these steps ensures you receive fair compensation and can focus on healing.
